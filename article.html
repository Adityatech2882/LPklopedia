Xero Demo Tape 2 1997

Before the world known as Linkin Park, the band began their journey by the name of Xero. One of the most interesting relics of the

early era was Xero Demo Tape (1997). A rare demo recording that became the foundation of their distinctive voice in the future.

Background

In the mid-1990s, Mike Shinoda and his friends formed Xero in California.These initial formations include Brad Delson, Rob Bourdon,

Joe Hahn, Dave Farrell, and lead vocalist Mark Wakefield. In 1997, they recorded a demo tape used to introduce their music to the record label.

This demo has not been commercially successful, but it is an important step dalam evolusi band.

Musical Styles & Characteristics

In the Xero era, their music has begun to show a unique identity:

  • Rap + Rock fusion by Mike Shinoda
  • Sampling & scratching from Joe Hahn
  • Riff heavy guitars affected by metal
  • Unspelled song structure albums such as Hybrid Theory

Transition to Linkin Park

After releasing the demo, Xero struggled to secure a record deal. A major turning point came when Mark

Wakefield left the band. They later recruited Chester Bennington, whose powerful and emotional vocals

transformed their sound.

The band went through name changes from Xero to Hybrid Theory, and finally to Linkin Park before

achieving worldwide success.
