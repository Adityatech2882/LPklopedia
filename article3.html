Xero Demo Tape A&R 1997

Xero (A&R Demo Tape) is one of the earliest known demo recordings by Xero, the band that would later

evolve into Linkin Park. Recorded in 1996 and released in 1997, this demo was specifically created to be sent

to record label executives as the band attempted to secure a recording contract.

Background

The demo originated from the early collaboration between Mike Shinoda and Mark Wakefield, who had been

friends since childhood. Their shared musical interests blended hip-hop and rock influences, which became

the foundation of Xero's sound.

After forming Xero around 1995-1996, they began writing songs and experimenting with recording. Guitarist

Brad Delson later joined to help record the demo, contributing guitar parts to several tracks.

The demo was recorded using a simple 4-track recorder in Mike Shinoda's bedroom, showcasing the band's

DIY approach during their early years.

Purpose and Industry Exposure

Unlike other early demos, this tape was specifically created as an A&R (Artists and Repertoire) submission.

The band sent it to record label representatives in hopes of getting signed.

One of the key moments came when an A&R representative from Immortal Records contacted them after

hearing the demo, impressed by the quality despite its low-budget production.

However, success did not come immediately—major labels reportedly passed on the band multiple times

before eventually offering a deal years later.

Track Listing

The demo contains four tracks:

  • Dialate
  • Fuse
  • Deftest
  • Stick N Move

Some of these songs would later evolve into different versions or influence future material in the band's

catalog.

Musical Style

The sound of A&R Demo Tape is raw and experimental, combining:

  • Hip-hop beats and rap vocals
  • Alternative rock and guitar-driven elements
  • Early attempts at genre fusion

Mike Shinoda handled much of the rapping and production, while Mark Wakefield provided melodic vocals.

Compared to later releases, the production is minimal, but it clearly shows the early blueprint of the band's

signature rap-rock style.

Personnel

The demo featured the early lineup of Xero:

  • Mike Shinoda
  • Mark Wakefield
  • Brad Delson

Rediscovery and Legacy

For many years, the existence of the A&R Demo Tape was largely unknown to the public. It only became

widely known in 2022, when a copy previously owned by a music industry executive surfaced and was

auctioned.

Today, the demo is considered an important piece of Linkin Park history. It documents the band at a time

when they were still developing their identity and struggling to break into the music industry.
